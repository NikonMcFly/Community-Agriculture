Agriculture is Good for Black Community

African Americans should look into owning and producing their food to their community. In this paper I will be talking about why it is great for young African Americans to own their own agriculture to supply their community, how healthy products could decrease obesity in the African Americans. Also, with the change in demand for more organic and quality of food, big food corporations are losing profits around customers change in taste.

As the decline of process food is taking a hit, African Americans are still amongst some of the highest obesity versus other race. To counter this attack, having many American Americans millenials grow and product food could decrease obesity and also give healthy and sustainable products to the community. Fast Food is a major part of African American youth diet. Most youth believe that fast food is not good for you but still eat it. African Americans youths can grow agriculture and could supply their local communities with quality. This is because healthy and organic food is not present at the store and in fast food resturants." Their is a huge change in customer taste and big corporations are scrambling to adapt to change in consumers taste. The top 5 food companies lost 18 billions dollars since 2009. Consumers are looking for more organic food.

Less millenials are farming which gives an increase in demand for natural food in the future. African Americans make up 2 percent of all the growers in America. If more African Americans farm, it will bring diversity in agriculture industry. Its also could bring better innovation, and more ideas about around the hospitality industry.

The demand for organic and quality of food for millenials, specially African Americans, would benefit more economically. Corporations are losing revenue around customers taste and are scrambling to find a better solution. African Americans are amongst the highest of obesity and the decline in revenue from corporation would supply better healthy products. The change of consumer taste and the increase in the demand for more organic and healthy food, would be a huge variable for the decrease of obesity.
